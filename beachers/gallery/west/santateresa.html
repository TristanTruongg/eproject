Santa Teresa beach

the Huge sandy beach of Santa Teresa lies on the shore of the village of Mal Pais which merges with the city of Santa Teresa in the South-Western part of the Nicoya Peninsula. Nearby, there is the great national Park Santa Teresa with the richest on the Pacific coast terrestrial and underwater flora and fauna.


Beach description

Santa Teresa Beach is considered to be one of the most beautiful places of Costa Rica. Fine light beige sand with a golden tint evenly covers kilometer-long sections of the coast bordering the splendid rainforest.

The entrance to the water is smooth, the bottom is sandy and rocky, but it is impossible to go slowly to the depth because of the powerful surf. Santa Theresa is not the best place for swimming, but is perfect for surfing. High waves and sea breezes provide excellent conditions for windsurfing.


Beach is always half-empty. Lone travelers, windsurfers and nudists like spending time here. No sunbeds or umbrellas. There are cheap hostels, campings and parking lots along the coastline. If you come to Santa Theresa with children, a hotel with a pool is the most suitable place for you.


There are no cafes or restaurants on the beach, you can have something in Mal País cafes.


The city of Santa Teresa has everything you need for a relaxing holiday - hotels, shops, supermarkets, restaurants, cafes, banks. You can rent a boat or speedboat for a walk along the beach, go to the waterfalls of Montezuma, in the protected area, or fishing.


You can get to Santa Theresa by a transit flight with the connection in New York, Mexico or Lima. The closest international airport to Santa Theresa is located in Tambor.
